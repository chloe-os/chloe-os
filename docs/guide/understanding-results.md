# Understanding Your Results

After running Chloe OS, you'll have an HTML report. Here's what each section means and what to do with it.

## Opening the Report

Double-click the HTML file or open it in any web browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox). The report is self-contained — no internet connection needed.

## Report Sections

### Disclaimer

The report starts with an important reminder: **this is a research tool, not medical advice.** Please read this section carefully.

### Executive Summary

A plain-language overview of what was found:

- How many mutations were found in your pet's tumor
- How many of those mutations could be potential vaccine targets
- The top candidates and why they stand out

### Top Neoantigen Candidates

This is the core of the report. It shows a table of the best potential vaccine targets, ranked by how likely they are to work. For each candidate:

- **Gene** — Which gene is affected by the mutation
- **Protein Change** — What the mutation does to the protein (e.g., "V600E" means position 600 changed from V to E)
- **Best Peptide** — The short protein fragment that could be a vaccine target
- **IC50** — How strongly the peptide binds to immune molecules (lower is better — under 500 is considered a "binder")
- **Score** — An overall score combining multiple factors

### Vet-Ready Summary

A section specifically formatted for sharing with your veterinarian. It summarizes:

- Key findings in clinical language
- What the candidates mean
- Suggested next steps
