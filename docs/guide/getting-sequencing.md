# Getting Your Pet's Tumor Sequenced

Before you can use Chloe OS, you need a VCF file containing the DNA mutations in your pet's tumor. Here's how to get one.

## What Is DNA Sequencing?

DNA sequencing reads the genetic code of cells. For cancer analysis, a lab reads the DNA from your pet's tumor and compares it to healthy tissue to find where the cancer's DNA has changed.

## What You Need

1. **A tumor sample** — Usually obtained during surgery or biopsy. Your veterinarian handles this.
2. **A normal tissue sample** (optional but recommended) — A blood draw or cheek swab from your pet. This helps distinguish true tumor mutations from your pet's normal genetic variations.

## How to Get Sequencing

### Step 1: Talk to Your Vet

Tell your veterinarian you're interested in tumor DNA sequencing. They can:

- Save tumor tissue during surgery (in formalin or flash-frozen)
- Arrange a biopsy if surgery isn't planned
- Collect a blood sample for normal comparison

### Step 2: Choose a Sequencing Provider

Several commercial labs offer tumor sequencing for animals:

- **Veterinary genomics labs** at university veterinary schools
- **Commercial sequencing services** that accept animal samples
- **Research collaborations** — some veterinary oncology researchers may be interested in partnering

!!! tip "What to ask the lab"
    Request **whole exome sequencing (WES)** or **whole genome sequencing (WGS)** of the tumor, with variant calling against either the CanFam reference genome or your pet's normal tissue. Ask for the results in **VCF format**.

### Step 3: Get Your VCF File

The lab will process the samples and return results. The key file you need is a **VCF (Variant Call Format)** file — this lists all the places where the tumor DNA differs from normal.

## Cost

Tumor DNA sequencing typically costs **$1,000–$5,000**, depending on:

- Sequencing depth and type (WES vs. WGS)
- Whether both tumor and normal are sequenced
- The lab and turnaround time

## Important Considerations

- **Talk to your vet first** — They need to be involved in sample collection
- **Timing matters** — Fresh or flash-frozen tissue gives better results than old preserved samples
- **This is just the first step** — Sequencing gives you data. Chloe OS helps you interpret it. But any treatment decisions require veterinary expertise.
