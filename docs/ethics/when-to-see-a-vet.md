# When to See a Vet

**The short answer: always.**

Chloe OS does not replace veterinary care at any stage. Here's when professional involvement is essential.

## Before Using Chloe OS

- **Diagnosis** — A veterinarian must diagnose the cancer and determine its type and stage
- **Treatment plan** — Standard treatments (surgery, chemotherapy, radiation) should be discussed first
- **Sample collection** — Tumor tissue for sequencing must be collected by a veterinarian
- **Prognosis** — Understanding your pet's outlook helps set realistic expectations

## While Using Chloe OS

- **Interpreting results** — A veterinary oncologist can help assess whether the identified neoantigens are clinically meaningful
- **Ongoing care** — Your pet's cancer doesn't pause while you run bioinformatics analyses

## After Getting Results

- **Reviewing the report** — Share the Vet-Ready Summary with your veterinarian
- **Deciding next steps** — Whether to pursue a personalized vaccine is a clinical decision
- **Finding a lab partner** — Your vet may have connections to research institutions
- **Treatment monitoring** — Any experimental treatment requires veterinary supervision

## Emergency Signs

Contact your veterinarian immediately if your pet shows:

- Rapid tumor growth
- Difficulty breathing, eating, or drinking
- Sudden lethargy or collapse
- Bleeding from tumors
- Signs of pain

**Do not delay veterinary care to wait for analysis results.**
